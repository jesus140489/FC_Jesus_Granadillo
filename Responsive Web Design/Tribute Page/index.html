Here's a time line of Diego Armando Maradona's life:

  • 1960 - Born in Villa Fiorito, a humble neighborhood on the outskirts of Buenos Aires. He is the fifth of eight children born to Diego Maradona and Dalma Salvadora Franco.
  • 1976 - Debuted in the Argentine championship with Argentinos Juniors against Talleres de CÃ³rdoba, ten days before his 16th birthday.
  • 1977 - Debuted in the Argentine team against Hungary.
  • 1981 - Joins Boca Juniors.
  • 1982 - Goes to Barcelona of Spain.
  • 1984 - Joins Napoli in Italy.
  • 1986 - Scores the famous goal with the "Hand of God" and then scores a second goal, defined by FIFA as the best in the world, in the 2-1 victory against England in the quarterfinals of the World Cup in Mexico.
  • 1986 - Leads the Argentine national team to victory with a 3-2 win over West Germany.
  • 1987 - Help Napoli win their first championship title.
  • 1990 - Leads Argentina to the World Cup final, where it loses 1-0 against Germany.
  • 1991 - Tests positive in an anti-doping control in Italy and is suspended for 15 months.
  • 1994 - Disputes the World Cup, but is positive in the second match for consuming ephedrine. At the request of FIFA, Argentina withdraws him from the tournament.
  • 1997 - Retires from football on the day of his 37th birthday.
  • 2000 - Suffers a cardiac crisis in Punta del Este, Uruguay, and is hospitalized in Buenos Aires, before traveling to Cuba where he will continue his recovery.
  • 2008 - Is appointed coach of the Argentina national team.
  • 2009 - Dies of cardiac arrest.
